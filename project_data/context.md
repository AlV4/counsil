# Founder Context

This file describes the founder for whom the SaaS Idea Council is evaluating ideas. Every advisor must take these constraints as fixed reality, not as variables to optimize away.

## Who I am

I am a backend developer with production experience on a real-time flight logistics system for the Milano Cortina 2026 Olympic and Paralympic Games. My day job is demanding and operationally serious — the system runs in production with real on-call pressure.

My core stack: Laravel / Lumen, Azure Kubernetes Service (AKS), Redis, RabbitMQ, MongoDB, MySQL, FrankenPHP, Swagger / OpenAPI, Guzzle, Eloquent. Strong areas: API design and integration, queue-based systems, finite state machines, database modeling, debugging distributed systems under load. I have done extensive work on logging, observability (Datadog), and Kubernetes deployments in real production conditions.

## My constraints — these are fixed, not negotiable

- **Time available for the side business:** 10–20 hours per week, around a full-time job that has on-call obligations. Advisors must assume this number cannot be increased without quitting the job.
- **Monthly experiment budget:** $50–200 USD per month total — covers infrastructure, tools, ads, subscriptions. Anything above this requires a financial decision, not a casual experiment.
- **I am fully solo.** No co-founder. No employees. No regular contractors. I may hire a freelancer for a one-off task, but advisors must NOT plan around team scaling.
- **English:** strong written, weaker on calls. Async communication (email, Slack, written demos, Loom videos) is comfortable. Live sales calls in English are friction — possible but not preferred at scale.
- **I will not become a content creator.** No daily Twitter, no YouTube channel, no TikTok. Distribution plans that depend on building a personal audience from zero are not a fit. Advisors should propose distribution that does not require this.

## My goals

- **Target:** $20K+ MRR within 18–24 months.
- **End state:** This SaaS becomes my primary income and replaces my full-time job. This is the expected path, not a maybe.
- This means the business must be capable of supporting one person fully — including taxes, healthcare, time off, and a buffer. A "lifestyle hobby" outcome is failure for me.

## My selling and customer interaction preferences

- I am willing to do **hybrid go-to-market**: hand-hold the first 10–30 customers manually (cold email, DMs, white-glove onboarding), then transition to self-serve as the product matures. I am NOT willing to run a high-touch sales-call business indefinitely.
- I prefer asynchronous, written, founder-led sales for early customers. Demo calls are acceptable but should not be the primary acquisition mechanic in the long run.

## Segments I exclude from consideration

- **Crypto / Web3.** Not interested — do not propose ideas in this space.
- **Generic AI wrappers (thin GPT-API layers with no defensible moat).** Not interested. AI as one component inside a deeper product is fine. AI as the entire product is not.

## Segments I am open to

I am open to any other segment. I have no strong attachment to a specific vertical. Some natural advantages worth noting (but not constraints):

- **Aviation / logistics / real-time operations:** I have working domain knowledge from the Olympics project. This gives me credibility and unfair speed in this vertical.
- **Developer tools / DevOps / API infrastructure:** I am the target user of these tools daily and understand the buyer.
- **Operationally serious B2B SaaS:** I am comfortable with systems that require correctness, observability, and reliability — many founders aren't.

These are advantages, not requirements. If a different segment fits the math better, I want advisors to say so.

## My known weaknesses as a founder

Advisors should keep these in mind when scoring operational and distribution viability:

- **Pricing discomfort.** I tend to underprice. Push back if I propose a number that destroys unit economics.
- **Over-preparation.** I tend to over-build before talking to users. Distribution and operational advisors should weight "talk to users this week" plans heavily.
- **No prior founder experience.** I have not sold a product to strangers before. This is a real skill gap, not a minor footnote.

## What "GO" means for me

A GO verdict from this council means:

1. The idea has a real, paying market that a solo founder can reach within 6 months.
2. Unit economics support a path to $20K+ MRR within 18–24 months at realistic conversion and churn rates.
3. Distribution does not require me to build a personal audience from zero.
4. Ongoing operational load fits inside 10–20 hours/week until at least $5K MRR, with a clear plan for scaling beyond that.
5. There is a defensible reason this product won't be commoditized within 12 months by a better-funded competitor or by a foundation-model release.

If any of these five conditions cannot be satisfied, the verdict should be PIVOT or NO-GO regardless of how interesting the idea is.
